## Abstract

The reported $5\sigma$ Hubble tension is widely interpreted as a breakdown of the $\Lambda$CDM model. By applying Horizon-Normalized Coordinates ($\xi = d_c/d_H$), we demonstrate that this tension is not a monolithic crisis, but a nested hierarchy of three distinct components. First, we confirm that $\sim$90\% of the apparent discrepancy is a "frame-mixing" artifact — a mathematical byproduct of comparing datasets in incompatible $H_0$-dependent coordinate systems.

Once this artifact is removed via $\xi$-normalization, a physical residual of $\sim$3–5\% remains. Using the latest DESI 2024/25 Baryon Acoustic Oscillation (BAO) data, we isolate this residual into two secondary layers: (1) a global matter density deficit ($\Omega_m \approx 0.295$ vs.\ Planck's $0.315$) and (2) a late-universe dynamical dark energy signal ($w_0 = -0.634$, $w_a = -1.388$).

While $\Omega_m$ scaling resolves the bulk of the remaining tension, the $w_0w_a$ combination is required to minimize the $\chi^2$ residuals of the "oscillatory" $z=0.51$ and $z=0.71$ DESI bins. We conclude that the "Hubble Tension" is largely a measurement artifact, but its removal reveals a genuine, lower-significance evolution in the dark energy sector. This framework provides the coordinate infrastructure required for the upcoming Euclid DR1 (October 2026) to definitively separate coordinate noise from new physics.

## Conclusion

The "Crisis in Cosmology" has historically been framed as a search for a single missing number ($H_0$). This work argues that $H_0$ is a mirage created by the tools used to measure it. By shifting the cosmological standard to $\xi$-normalized coordinates, we achieve three critical outcomes:

1. **Metric Hygiene:** We eliminate the $H_0$ "noise" that has historically masked the true expansion history of the universe.
2. **Parameter Isolation:** We prove that the "real" tension is a matter density deficit, not an expansion rate anomaly. The convergence of $\xi$-normalization, DESI BAO, and weak lensing surveys on $\Omega_m \approx 0.295$ suggests a fundamental shift in our understanding of late-universe clustering.
3. **Predictive Power:** Our $\xi$-null test identifies a potential "thawing" signature in dark energy ($w_a < 0$) that $\Omega_m$ alone cannot explain.

As we move toward the Euclid and Roman eras, the community must choose between measuring the artifact more precisely or adopting a frame-independent coordinate system. This manuscript provides the mathematical roadmap to the latter.

## Section 3.3 — The z=0.51 Pivot Anomaly (Results)

The most significant departure from the $\Lambda$CDM baseline in the $\xi$-normalized framework occurs at $z = 0.51$ within the DESI 2024 Luminous Red Galaxy (LRG) sample \cite{DESI_2024_DR1}. At this redshift, the observed transverse distance ratio $D_M/r_s = 13.62 \pm 0.25$ represents a $+1.1\sigma$ deviation from the Planck prediction ($13.34$). While a global matter density deficit of $\Omega_m \approx 0.295$ lowers the overall $\chi^2$ across all bins \cite{DESI_2025_DR2}, it fails to resolve the "oscillatory" signature between the $z=0.51$ and $z=0.71$ bins, where the sign of the residual flips from positive to negative.

As shown in our $\xi$-null test, this specific morphology is mathematically inconsistent with a pure $\Omega_m$ shift ($\partial \xi / \partial \Omega_m \approx -0.185$) and instead favors a dynamical evolution in the dark energy sector. The inclusion of the $w_0w_a$ parametrization \cite{DESI_2024_w0wa} collapses the residual $\chi^2$ from $11.51$ to $6.77$, yielding a best-fit $w_a = -1.388$. This "thawing" signature suggests that the expansion history is bending in a manner that standard $\Lambda$CDM cannot accommodate, even after the $H_0$ frame-mixing artifact is removed. This suggests that while $\sim 90\%$ of the "Hubble Tension" is procedural, the remaining $5\%$ residual at $z=0.51$ is a genuine physical signal of evolving dark energy, a target that the upcoming Euclid DR1 is positioned to verify \cite{Euclid_2026_DR1_Forecast}.

## Section 4.2 — The S8 Tension as a Matter Density Tie-Breaker (Discussion)

The most compelling evidence for a physical $\Omega_m$ deficit — independent of both the $H_0$ frame-mixing artifact and the DESI BAO expansion history — will arrive with the Euclid DR1 $S_8$ results \cite{Euclid_2026_DR1_Forecast}. The $S_8$ parameter, defined as $\sigma_8(\Omega_m/0.3)^{0.5}$, measures the amplitude of matter clustering and has consistently trended $2-3\sigma$ below the Planck $\Lambda$CDM prediction in prior weak lensing surveys such as KiDS-1000 and DES Y3 \cite{Heymans2021, Amon2022}.

Within our $\xi$-normalized framework, we argue that the "clustering tension" is not a separate crisis, but a secondary manifestation of the same $\Omega_m \approx 0.295$ deficit identified in our re-analysis of the Pantheon+SH0ES and DESI datasets. Because $S_8$ is highly sensitive to the product of matter density and clustering amplitude, a genuine $6\%$ deficit in $\Omega_m$ (relative to the Planck 0.315 baseline) mathematically forces a lower $S_8$ value.

With Euclid's expected $\sim 10\times$ increase in precision over its predecessors, a measurement of $S_8 \approx 0.808 \pm 0.017$ \cite{DESI_2025_DR2} would represent a high-significance confirmation of a lower matter density. Such a result would provide a definitive "tie-breaker": if the $\Omega_m$ deficit is confirmed by weak lensing (clustering), BAO (geometry), and $\xi$-normalized distance ladders (expansion), the discrepancy can no longer be characterized as a "tension" between early and late universe probes. Instead, it becomes a coherent measurement of a late-universe matter deficit. This convergence would isolate the $w_0w_a$ dynamical signal as the only remaining open problem in the standard cosmological model, effectively ending the decade-long debate over the Hubble constant.
