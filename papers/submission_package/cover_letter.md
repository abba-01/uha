Dear Editor,

I am submitting the manuscript "The Hubble Tension as a Measurement Artifact: Quantifying the Frame-Mixing Systematic via Horizon-Normalized Coordinates" for consideration as a Letter in Monthly Notices of the Royal Astronomical Society.

This manuscript addresses the long-standing Hubble tension by identifying and quantifying a systematic coordinate-frame incompatibility that substantially inflates the reported discrepancy. Using the publicly available Pantheon+SH0ES dataset, the work demonstrates that 93.3% of the apparent H₀ tension dissolves when distances are expressed in horizon-normalized coordinates, and that the residual physical signal is a matter density discrepancy (Ω_m ≈ 0.295–0.304 vs Planck 0.315) confirmed independently by DESI BAO measurements and S₈/σ₈ weak lensing surveys.

The manuscript is 5 pages in length, consistent with the MNRAS Letters format. All analysis code and data are publicly available via GitHub (github.com/abba-01/uha) and the results are fully reproducible from the public Pantheon+SH0ES release. The work has been deposited on Zenodo (DOI: 10.5281/zenodo.19211662).

I have no conflicts of interest to declare. The manuscript has not been submitted elsewhere and is not under consideration at any other journal.

I have suggested reviewers with expertise in cosmological distance measurements and systematic biases. I request that the following individuals be excluded due to direct competing interests in the Hubble tension measurement landscape: Prof. Adam Riess (SH0ES Principal Investigator) and Dr. Vivian Poulin (Early Dark Energy modelling).

Thank you for your consideration.
