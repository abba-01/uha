Dear Editor,

I am submitting the manuscript "The Hubble Tension as a Measurement Artifact II: Isolating the Ω_m Deficit and w_a Evolution via Horizon-Normalized Coordinates" for consideration as a Letter in Monthly Notices of the Royal Astronomical Society.

This work is the direct successor to our previous MNRAS Letters submission (Paper I, under review, DOI: 10.5281/zenodo.19211662), which demonstrated that 93.3% of the Hubble tension is a frame-mixing artifact of incompatible coordinate assumptions. In this follow-up, we apply the Horizon-Normalized Coordinate (ξ) framework to the DESI 2024/2025 BAO dataset to characterise the full physical structure of the residual.

Our findings represent a significant advance in the cosmological picture:

- We confirm a ~4–6% matter density deficit (Ω_m ≈ 0.295) consistent across three independent probes: ξ-normalized distance ladders (this work), DESI BAO geometry (DR1 and DR2), and S₈/σ₈ weak lensing surveys (KiDS-1000, DES Y3).
- We identify a secondary dynamical dark energy signal (w_a ≈ −1.388, Δχ² = 4.74) in the DESI pivot bins that is only statistically visible once the H₀ coordinate noise is removed by ξ-normalization.
- We demonstrate that the JWST 8σ result does not contradict these findings, as our results emerge from H₀-independent BAO geometry rather than the local distance ladder.

Given the imminent release of Euclid DR1 in October 2026 — the first dataset with sufficient precision to independently confirm or refute the Ω_m deficit — this paper provides the coordinate infrastructure the community needs to interpret those results without systematic frame-mixing contamination.

All analysis code is publicly available at github.com/abba-01/uha. The manuscript has not been submitted elsewhere and is not under consideration at any other journal. I have no conflicts of interest to declare.
