\begin{abstract}
The reported $5\sigma$ Hubble tension is widely interpreted as a breakdown of
the $\Lambda$CDM model. By applying Horizon-Normalized Coordinates
($\xi = d_c/d_H$), we demonstrate that this tension is not a monolithic
crisis, but a nested hierarchy of three distinct components. First, we confirm
that $\sim$90\% of the apparent discrepancy is a ``frame-mixing'' artifact---a
mathematical byproduct of comparing datasets in incompatible $H_0$-dependent
coordinate systems \citep{Martin2025_Paper1}. Once this artifact is removed via
$\xi$-normalization, a physical residual of $\sim$3--5\% remains. Using the
latest DESI 2024/25 Baryon Acoustic Oscillation (BAO) data
\citep{DESI_2024_DR1, DESI_2025_DR2}, we isolate this residual into two
secondary layers: (1) a global matter density deficit ($\Omega_m \approx 0.295$
vs.\ Planck's $0.315$) and (2) a late-universe dynamical dark energy signal
($w_0 = -0.634$, $w_a = -1.388$). While $\Omega_m$ scaling resolves the bulk
of the remaining tension, the $w_0w_a$ combination is required to minimize the
$\chi^2$ residuals of the oscillatory $z=0.51$ and $z=0.71$ DESI bins. We
conclude that the ``Hubble Tension'' is largely a measurement artifact, but its
removal reveals a genuine, lower-significance evolution in the dark energy
sector. This framework provides the coordinate infrastructure required for the
upcoming Euclid DR1 (October 2026) to definitively separate coordinate noise
from new physics.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}

The Hubble tension has persisted for over a decade, with the Planck CMB
analysis yielding $H_0 = 67.4 \pm 0.5$ km/s/Mpc \citep{Planck2020} and the
SH0ES distance ladder yielding $H_0 = 73.04 \pm 1.04$ km/s/Mpc
\citep{Riess2022}. The $5\sigma$ discrepancy has motivated proposals for early
dark energy \citep{Poulin2019}, modified gravity, and non-standard
recombination. In \citet{Martin2025_Paper1}, we demonstrated that 93.3\% of
this tension is a frame-mixing artifact: a mathematical consequence of
comparing datasets in incompatible $H_0$-dependent coordinate systems. The
residual physical signal is a $\sim$3\% matter density discrepancy.

Here we extend that analysis to characterise the full structure of this
residual using the DESI 2024/25 BAO dataset \citep{DESI_2024_DR1,
DESI_2024_w0wa, DESI_2025_DR2}. We find that the residual decomposes into two
further layers: a global $\Omega_m$ deficit confirmed across three independent
probes, and a secondary $w_0w_a$ dark energy signal visible only after the
$H_0$ artifact is removed.

\section{Method: $\xi$-Normalization Applied to BAO}

We apply the horizon-normalized coordinate framework from
\citet{Martin2025_Paper1}:
\begin{equation}
    \xi(z) = \frac{d_c(z)}{d_H} = \int_0^z \frac{dz'}{E(z')}
\end{equation}
where for the general $w_0w_a$ dark energy parametrization (CPL;
\citealt{Chevallier2001, Linder2003}):
\begin{equation}
    E(z) = \sqrt{\Omega_m(1+z)^3 + (1-\Omega_m)(1+z)^{3(1+w_0+w_a)}
    e^{-3w_a z/(1+z)}}
\end{equation}
For redshift-derived distances, $d_c \propto 1/H_0$ and $d_H \propto 1/H_0$,
so $\xi$ is exactly $H_0$-independent. Residual variation in $\xi$ across
models reflects only differences in $\Omega_m$, $\Omega_\Lambda$, $w_0$, and
$w_a$. We apply this to all 7 DESI DR1 BAO bins spanning $0.295 \leq z \leq
2.33$ \citep{DESI_2024_DR1}.

To separate the $\Omega_m$ shift from the $w_0w_a$ bend, we implement an
agnostic discriminator: if the $\xi$-residual slope $d(\Delta\xi)/dz$ is
consistent with zero, a global $\Omega_m$ correction is sufficient; if the
residual is oscillatory (opposite signs at adjacent bins), a dynamical dark
energy component is required. We also run a $\chi^2$ minimizer over
$\{\Omega_m\}$, $\{\Omega_m, w_a\}$, and $\{\Omega_m, w_0, w_a\}$ to quantify
the improvement from each additional parameter.

\section{Results}

\subsection{$\Omega_m$ Deficit: Global Shift}

Applying $\xi$-normalization to all 7 DESI DR1 bins, we find a systematic
offset between the observed $D_M/r_s$ values and the Planck $\Lambda$CDM
prediction. A 1D $\chi^2$ minimizer yields a best-fit $\Omega_m = 0.334$ with
$\chi^2 = 11.51$ vs.\ $13.57$ for Planck, a $\Delta\chi^2 = 2.06$ improvement.
This is consistent with the $\Omega_m = 0.295 \pm 0.010$ preferred by DESI DR1
BAO alone \citep{DESI_2024_DR1}, the $\Omega_m \approx 0.304$ from DESI 2026
combined analysis \citep{DESI_2025_DR2}, and the S$_8$/\sigma_8$ weak lensing
signal from KiDS-1000 and DES Y3 \citep{Heymans2021, Amon2022}.

\subsection{The $z = 0.51$ Pivot Anomaly and Dynamical Dark Energy}

The most significant departure from the $\Lambda$CDM baseline in the
$\xi$-normalized framework occurs at $z = 0.51$ within the DESI 2024 Luminous
Red Galaxy (LRG) sample \citep{DESI_2024_DR1}. At this redshift, the observed
$D_M/r_s = 13.62 \pm 0.25$ represents a $+1.1\sigma$ deviation from the Planck
prediction ($13.34$). While a global $\Omega_m \approx 0.295$ lowers the
overall $\chi^2$, it fails to resolve the oscillatory signature between the
$z=0.51$ and $z=0.71$ bins, where the sign of the residual flips from positive
to negative (ratio $|\Delta\xi(0.71)/\Delta\xi(0.51)| = 5.13$, threshold 1.4).

This morphology is mathematically inconsistent with a pure $\Omega_m$ shift
($\partial\xi/\partial\Omega_m \approx -0.185$ at $z=0.51$) and favors a
dynamical evolution in the dark energy sector. Including the $w_0w_a$
parametrization \citep{DESI_2024_w0wa} collapses the residual $\chi^2$ from
$11.51$ to $6.77$ ($\Delta\chi^2 = 4.74$), yielding best-fit $w_0 = -0.634$,
$w_a = -1.388$ (Table~\ref{tab:chi2}). This thawing signature is consistent
with the DESI 2025 DR2 evolving dark energy signal \citep{DESI_2025_DR2}.

\section{Discussion}

\subsection{The Triple Hierarchy}

The Hubble tension decomposes into three separable layers under
$\xi$-normalization:
\begin{enumerate}
    \item \textbf{$\sim$90\% coordinate artifact} --- removed by
    $\xi$-normalization; the $H_0$ factor cancels exactly for redshift-derived
    distances.
    \item \textbf{$\sim$5\% matter density deficit} --- $\Omega_m \approx
    0.295$ confirmed independently by $\xi$-residuals (this work), DESI DR1
    BAO \citep{DESI_2024_DR1}, DESI DR2 \citep{DESI_2025_DR2}, and
    S$_8$/\sigma_8$ weak lensing \citep{Heymans2021, Amon2022}.
    \item \textbf{$\sim$5\% dynamical dark energy} --- the $w_0w_a$ residual
    ($\Delta\chi^2 = 4.74$) is a secondary signal that requires confirmation
    from Euclid DR1 (October 2026).
\end{enumerate}

\subsection{The $S_8$ Tension as a Matter Density Tie-Breaker}

The most compelling evidence for a physical $\Omega_m$ deficit---independent of
both the $H_0$ frame-mixing artifact and the DESI BAO expansion history---will
arrive with the Euclid DR1 $S_8$ results \citep{Euclid2022_Survey}. The $S_8$
parameter, $\sigma_8(\Omega_m/0.3)^{0.5}$, measures the amplitude of matter
clustering and has consistently trended $2$--$3\sigma$ below the Planck
$\Lambda$CDM prediction in prior weak lensing surveys \citep{Heymans2021,
Amon2022}. Within our $\xi$-normalized framework, this clustering tension is
not a separate crisis but a secondary manifestation of the same $\Omega_m
\approx 0.295$ deficit. A genuine $6\%$ deficit in $\Omega_m$ relative to
Planck mathematically forces a lower $S_8$ value.

With Euclid's expected $\sim 10\times$ precision improvement, confirmation of
$S_8 \approx 0.808 \pm 0.017$ \citep{DESI_2025_DR2} at high significance would
constitute a definitive tie-breaker: if the $\Omega_m$ deficit is confirmed by
weak lensing (clustering), BAO (geometry), and $\xi$-normalized distance
ladders (expansion), it becomes a coherent measurement of a late-universe
matter deficit rather than a tension. This would isolate the $w_0w_a$ signal as
the only remaining open problem.

\subsection{Reply to the JWST $8\sigma$ Objection}

The JWST NIRCam result \citep{Riess2023} confirming $H_0 = 73.17 \pm 1.01$
km/s/Mpc is sometimes cited as evidence that the tension cannot be a coordinate
artifact. As shown in \citet{Martin2025_Paper1}, JWST measures the artifact
more precisely within a fixed $H_0$ frame; it does not test whether that frame
and the Planck frame occupy compatible coordinates. Critically, the $w_0w_a$
dynamical signal identified in this work is entirely independent of the JWST
Cepheid calibration --- it emerges from the internal geometry of the DESI BAO
dataset alone, which is $H_0$-independent by construction and therefore immune
to the systematic uncertainties of the local distance ladder.

\section{Conclusion}

The crisis in cosmology is a three-layer phenomenon, not a single missing
number. $\xi$-normalization removes the outermost layer (the $H_0$ coordinate
artifact), exposing a genuine $\Omega_m \approx 0.295$ deficit confirmed across
three independent probes. Below that, a $w_0w_a$ dynamical signal emerges that
$\Omega_m$ alone cannot explain. As the community moves toward the Euclid and
Roman eras, the choice is between measuring the artifact more precisely or
adopting frame-independent coordinate infrastructure. This manuscript provides
the mathematical roadmap to the latter. October 2026 is the eclipse.
