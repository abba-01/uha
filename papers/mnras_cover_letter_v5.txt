WHAT THIS PAPER CLAIMS — AND DOES NOT CLAIM

I wish to be direct about the central argument, as it is easily
misread. This paper does not claim that a coordinate transformation
changes any physical observable. It claims that the standard comparison
procedure — computing the tension as
(H0_SH0ES - H0_Planck) / sigma_combined — treats two best-fit
parameters from different cosmological frameworks as if they were
independent measurements of the same quantity. They are not.

H0_SH0ES = 73.04 km/s/Mpc is extracted from the Hubble diagram
(Riess et al. 2022, Section 2) under a cosmological model with
Omega_m = 0.334. H0_Planck = 67.4 km/s/Mpc is extracted from the
CMB acoustic scale (Planck Collaboration 2020, Section 5.4, Table 2)
under a model with Omega_m = 0.315. Since the luminosity distance
d_L(z; H0, Omega_m) depends jointly on both parameters (Eq. 2 of this
manuscript), the extracted H0 values absorb part of the Omega_m
difference. The 5-sigma figure quantifies the combined effect of this
frame difference plus any genuine physical discrepancy — it does not
separate them.

The horizon-normalized coordinate xi = d_c/d_H achieves this
separation. The H0 factors in d_c and d_H cancel exactly for any
redshift-derived distance (proven analytically in Eq. 6 of this
manuscript), leaving a residual that depends only on Omega_m and
other shape parameters. This is not a redefinition — it is a
projection onto the subspace of distance space from which H0 has
been genuinely removed.

SPECIFIC ADDITIONS IN THIS MANUSCRIPT

This manuscript has been substantially restructured relative to any
prior version to address the following points:

1. Section 2 (new) explicitly identifies the frame-mixing error
   with reference to specific equations in Riess et al. 2022 and
   Planck Collaboration 2020 — pointing to the exact terms in which
   the incompatible Omega_m assumptions enter the H0 extraction.

2. Section 4 (new) provides a complete statistical methodology:
   adaptive quadrature computation of d_c, the artifact fraction
   formula (Eq. 9) with bootstrap uncertainty (10^4 iterations),
   and explicit derivation of the Cepheid-per-object H0 via
   Eq. 10. The larger Cepheid uncertainty (13.97 vs. 1.04 km/s/Mpc)
   is explained in full: it is the sample standard deviation across
   43 independently analyzed calibrators, not a pipeline fit
   parameter. These are fundamentally different quantities and the
   larger value is the statistically honest one.

3. The result table now references the equations used to derive
   each entry.

4. The manuscript uses MNRAS document class formatting throughout.

SIGNIFICANCE

The paper's primary contribution is methodological: it demonstrates
that 93.3% (+/- 0.1%) of the apparent Hubble tension is a
coordinate-frame artifact quantifiable on existing public data,
and isolates a real 3% physical residual attributable to an Omega_m
discrepancy independently confirmed by DESI BAO DR1/DR2 and S8
weak lensing surveys. If correct, this substantially reduces the
required energy injection in Early Dark Energy models and reframes
the cosmological tension problem as primarily a matter-density
question rather than an expansion-rate question.
