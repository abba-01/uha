## Phase 1: The Foundation Layer (June 2025)

**The work starts with observation, not cosmology.**

The earliest recorded sessions (June 8-29, 2025) are about the Meta Modal Platform
(MMP) — a framework for classifying anomalous observations. The core problem being
solved: how do you encode an observation in a way that is frame-independent,
reproducible, and auditable? How do you separate what was seen from how it was seen?

Key sessions:
- **2025-06-21**: "Anomalous Phenomena Classification Framework" — first systematic
  attempt to build a taxonomy that doesn't depend on the observer's prior assumptions.
- **2025-06-22**: "Autonomous Alien Theory Research Paper" (60 msgs) — the theoretical
  root: if something observes itself, what coordinate system does it use?
- **2025-06-25**: "Post-Biological Intelligence Research Framework" — SAID framework
  emerges: AI must disclose its reasoning chain for science to trust it.
- **2025-06-27**: "Orbital Observation Framework Design" — spatial addressing as a
  solution to the observer-dependence problem. First trace of what will become UHA.

**The thread:** How do you compare observations made from different frames without
assuming a shared reference? This question, asked about UAP/anomalous phenomena,
is the same question the Hubble tension is about — answered four months later.

## The Logical Thread

Every piece of this work traces back to one question asked in June 2025:

> How do you compare observations made from different frames without
> assuming a shared reference?

The answer built out in layers:
1. **MMP/OMMP** — taxonomy for frame-independent observation (June 2025)
2. **AIWARED/eBIOS** — AI must disclose its observational assumptions (July 2025)
3. **UHA** — spatial encoding that doesn't inherit H0 (August 2025)
4. **N/U Algebra** — uncertainty propagation that doesn't assume correlation (Sep-Oct 2025)
5. **xi-normalization** — apply UHA to cosmological distances (October 2025)
6. **Hubble tension partition** — apply everything to the full tension landscape (March 2026)

The cosmology paper is not where the work starts. It is where it arrives.
