## Why "fold" is the wrong term

a) **Folding implies operating on the manifold.** Alcubierre warp drives, traversable wormholes — these all assume spacetime is the substrate and you can manipulate its topology directly. They require exotic matter, negative energy, geometric stresses. They violate energy conditions or require unobserved physics.

b) **Going through implies operating beneath the manifold.** You don't manipulate spacetime; you descend to the layer where spacetime is emergent and isn't a constraint. The substrate is what's real; spacetime is its high-layer expression.

c) **The Fermi paradox prediction differs:** folding-space FTL would have detectable drive signatures (massive energy expenditure, geometric distortions). Substrate-level traversal would have NO high-layer signature because the operation isn't a high-layer event. **Advanced civilizations using rail traversal would be invisible to our sky surveys by mechanism, not by choice.** This is consistent with what we actually observe (60+ years of null technosignature surveys).
